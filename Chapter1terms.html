Three Misconceptions about Computer Science

  1. Computer science is the study of computers
  2. Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs
  3. Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software

Briefly Define:

  1. Computer science: The study of algorithms, including their mathematical properties, hardware, and linguistic realizations and their applications.
  2. Algorithms: A step-by-step set of instructions for solving a problem or performing a task.
  3. Sequential Operation: A process where instructions are executed one after another in a fixed order.
  4. Conditional Operation: Question-based instructions in an algorithm; the next step depends on circumstances.
  5. Iterative Operation: "Looping" instructions of an algorithm. Repeat previous instructions until an intended result is acquired.
  6. Stored program computer: A computer that keeps both programs and data in its memory.
  7. Programmer: A person who writes and tests code.
  8. Integrated circuit: A small electronic chip combining many parts like switches and wires to perform a specific task, e.g., a smartphone.

Briefly describe these computer generations:

  1. First generation (1940s-1950s): Used vacuum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory; large, slow, and generated a lot of heat.
  2. Second generation (1950s-1960s): Used transistors replacing vacuum tubes; smaller and faster.
  3. Third generation (1960s-1970s): Used integrated circuits; more reliable, more powerful, and efficient. This era saw the development of operating systems and keyboards/monitors.
  4. Fourth generation (1970s-1980s): Used microprocessors; personal computers emerged. Rise of GUIs, the mouse, and development of the internet.
  5. Fifth generation (1990-present): Uses artificial intelligence and advanced computing technologies, including quantum computing; focused on systems that learn and adapt.

FORTRAN:

  1. What does the acronym FORTRAN stand for?

    Formula translation

  2. When was FORTRAN developed?

    1957

  3. What was it used for?

    Scientific and engineering calculations

  4. Is FORTRAN still being used today? If so, what for?

    Yes, for advanced scientific computations and simulations

COBOL:

  1. What does the acronym COBOL stand for?

    Common business-oriented language

  2. When was COBOL developed?

    1959

  3. What was it used for?

    Business, finance, and administrative systems

  4. Is COBOL still being used today? If so, what for?

    Yes, in legacy banking and government systems
