Chapter 7 Key Terms (Table)

Concise, study-ready definitions.

Networking concepts TermDefinition LANLocal Area Network; connects devices over a small area (home, lab, building). MANCity-scale network that links multiple LANs. WANWide Area Network spanning large regions (e.g., carrier links, the Internet). TopologyPhysical/logical layout of a network (bus, ring, star). BusAll nodes share one main communication line. RingNodes connect in a loop; messages circulate around the ring. StarNodes connect to a central switch/hub; easy to manage. PacketUnit of data with headers and payload. ProtocolRules for formatting, sending, and receiving data. EthernetDominant LAN tech; modern switched Ethernet avoids collisions. RouterForwards packets between networks using IP addresses/routes. SwitchForwards frames inside a LAN using MAC addresses. TCP/IPInternet suite; TCP = reliable transport, IP = addressing/routing. DNSTranslates hostnames into IP addresses. FirewallFilters traffic based on security rules.

Layered Networking (Ordered List)

  1. Application — HTTP, DNS, SMTP.
  2. Transport — TCP (reliable) / UDP (fast).
  3. Internet — IP routing, ICMP.
  4. Link — Ethernet, Wi-Fi.
  5. Physical — copper, fiber, RF.

IEEE — Questions & Answers

What does “IEEE” stand for?
Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers.
Mission & vision?

Mission: Advance technology for the benefit of humanity.

Vision (summary): Be essential to the global technical community and recognized for improving global conditions through technology and professionals.

Benefits of membership?
  • Global community, networking, mentorship.
  • Access to resources and member discounts (conferences, publications, learning).
  • Opportunities to engage in standards and local Sections/Societies.
Student membership — requirements & annual cost (USA)?
  • Requirements: Undergraduate/graduate carrying at least 50% of a full-time load in an IEEE-designated field.
  • Typical USA student dues: around US$42 for a full year (check current rates on the IEEE site).
